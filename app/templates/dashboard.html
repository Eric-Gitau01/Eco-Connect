Deforestation Threats in Karura Forest: A Call for Conservation

Karura Forest, Nairobi, Kenya


Karura Forest, one of Nairobi’s largest urban green spaces, faces growing deforestation risks from illegal logging, urban encroachment, and land-use changes. Despite its protected status, unauthorized activities threaten its biodiversity, water catchment role, and recreational value. The forest’s 1,041 hectares host endangered species and serve as a critical carbon sink. Community-led conservation efforts, alongside strict enforcement, are vital to curb habitat loss. Sustainable urban planning and public awareness can help preserve this ecological treasure. Immediate action is needed to balance development with environmental protection.
